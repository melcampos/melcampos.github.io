Research Focus

As a population geneticist, my scientific passion lies in understanding natural populations and their evolutionary dynamics. My research focuses on medical entomology, with a particular commitment to bridging the gap between basic science and applied vector control. I strongly believe that effective vector control must be informed by robust scientific evidence, and that translating research into practical, field-relevant solutions is imperative.

Laboratory

Extensive experience with genomic and transcriptomic library preparation for Illumina platforms; mosquito rearing and manipulation (dissections, embryo/adult microinjections), and experimental infections. These wet-lab capabilities tightly couple with downstream population-genomic analyses to close the loop from sample to inference.

Field

Field collections across urban and rural settings in Brazil and Africa, sampling multiple life stages and species (Aedes, Anopheles) using purpose-specific methods. Emphasis on geo-referenced designs that enable fine-scale inference on dispersal, connectivity, and local adaptation relevant to control programs.

Bioinformatics

Population-genomics pipelines built in R, Python, and Unix/HPC environments; from classic population-genetic toolkits to modern workflows for demographic modeling, selection scans, phasing, and resistance mapping. Strong interest in reproducible, open workflows that connect data science, clear visualization, and science communication.
