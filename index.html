Population Genetics of Mosquitoes

I hold the position of Project Scientist at the University of California, Davis. I earned my PhD in Genetics in 2016 and have since dedicated my academic pursuits to the field of population genetics, particularly focusing on mosquito vectors that transmit human diseases.

My Research

My research revolves around field-collected specimens, requiring a comprehensive understanding of various mosquito aspects, about their biology, ecology, and behavior. The integration of genetic insights into vector dynamics with an understanding of natural populations holds immense value for public health initiatives and vector control strategies. This is exactly what drives and guides my research, bridging the gap between academic inquiry and practical control measures. My particular focus centers on delving into population attributes including structure, dispersal, divergence, and demographic trends. I am intrigued by the interplay of these attributes with phenotypic traits such as resistance to insecticides and parasites, as well as mosquito behaviors related to feeding, resting, and mating preferences.

Laboratory

I have extensive expertise in genomic and transcriptomic library preparations for sequencing employing Illumina technology. Furthermore, I have also acquired significant hands-on experience in mosquito rearing and handling techniques, including delicate procedures like dissection, micro-injection of both adult mosquitoes and eggs, and the infection of mosquitoes with the malaria parasite.

Field

I have accumulated field work experience by collecting various mosquito species from the Aedes and Anopheles genus, using diverse collection methods tailored for distinct purposes and mosquito life stages. These collections have taken place across both urban and rural environments, spanning locations in Brazil and Africa.

Bioinformatics

I acquired my bioinformatics skills by meeting the specific requirements of my research. My journey encompasses a range of experiences, starting from earlier generations of software designed for population genetics to navigating server environments and embracing High-Performance Computing. I have delved deeply into a variety of programming languages, such as R, Python, and Unix. As a result, I became quite independent and skilled bioinformatician, proficiently wielding numerous tools for population genomic analysis.
